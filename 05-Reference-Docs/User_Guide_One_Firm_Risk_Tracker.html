What is the One Firm Risk Tracker?

The One Firm Risk Tracker is an interactive web dashboard that gives the OFR team a single, shared view of all open issues with built-in staleness tracking, update history, and weekly review metrics. It drives the full issue lifecycle — from identification through active management to resolution.

Getting Started

Open the Risk Tracker in your browser. You'll see the sign-in screen with two options: sign in with your PwC account, or try the demo.

The login screen with PwC Microsoft sign-in and Demo Mode access

How to sign in

1

Click "Sign in with Microsoft" to authenticate with your PwC account. You may be prompted for multi-factor authentication (MFA).

2

Once signed in, click "Connect to SharePoint" to load your team's live risk data. A green Connected badge will appear in the header.

3

Alternatively, click "Try Demo" to explore the tool with sample data — no sign-in required. An amber Demo Mode badge indicates you're using sample data.

The header bar displays your connection status and language toggle
You can switch between Demo Mode and Live Mode at any time by signing in or out. Demo Mode is useful for training new team members without affecting real data.

Weekly Review Dashboard

The top of the screen shows a real-time summary at a glance — designed for the weekly committee review.

The dashboard with KPI summary cards, intake panel, and issue tracker

What the numbers mean

Open Items

Total number of risks/issues currently being tracked (excludes Closed items).

Stale

Items that haven't been updated in 14+ days. Shown in red to flag attention.

High Priority

Number of items marked as High priority that are still open.

Medium / Low

Breakdown of remaining items by priority level.

Below the KPI cards, you'll see a status breakdown showing how many items are Active, Monitoring, Escalated, or New.

Submitting New Issues

The Issue Intake panel is where new risks and issues enter the system. Items submitted here go into a triage queue for committee review before being added to the formal register.

The Issue Intake section with pending items shown as cards

Submitting a new issue

1

Click the "New Issue" button in the top-right of the Intake section.

2

Fill in the title, owner (who is responsible), priority (High, Medium, or Low), and an optional description.

3

Click Submit. The item appears as a card in the intake queue.

What happens next

Each intake card has two actions:

  • Move to Tracker — promotes the item into the formal issue tracker. It gets a tracking ID (e.g., OFR-009) and enters the active lifecycle.
  • Dismiss (X) — removes the item from the queue if it's not relevant or a duplicate.
Only committee members with the appropriate permissions can promote or dismiss intake items. Regular users can submit issues for review.

Issue Tracker

The main table is the heart of the tool — a sortable, filterable view of all tracked issues with real-time staleness indicators and full lifecycle history.

The Issue Tracker showing all actively managed items

Understanding the columns

ID

Unique tracking number (OFR-001, OFR-002, etc.) assigned when an item enters the register.

Topic

Brief description of the risk or issue being tracked.

Owner

The person responsible for managing and updating this item.

Priority

Severity level: High Medium Low

Status

Current state: Active New Escalated Monitoring

Days Since Update

Color-coded staleness indicator. Green = recent, amber = aging, red = overdue.

Staleness indicators

The Days Since Update column uses color coding to flag items that need attention:

Color-coded staleness: green (recent), amber (aging), red (overdue), with pulse animation on critical items
Green (0-6 days) — Recently updated
Amber (7-13 days) — Aging, consider updating
Red (14+ days) — Stale, update required

Expanding a row

Click the arrow icon on the left side of any row to expand it. The expanded view shows the complete Update History — every status change and note that's been recorded for that item, displayed as a timeline.

An expanded row revealing the full update history with timestamps and status changes

Adding Updates

Keeping items up to date is essential. The update workflow lets you add notes and optionally change an item's status in one step.

The Add Update dialog with notes field and optional status change

How to add an update

1

Expand the row by clicking the arrow icon on the left side of the item you want to update.

2

Click the pencil icon (Add Update) that appears in the expanded section. This opens the update dialog.

3

Type your update notes in the text area. Be specific about what's changed, what actions were taken, or what's planned next.

4

(Optional) Use the Change Status dropdown to move the item to a different status — for example, from "Active" to "Monitoring" or "Escalated".

5

Click "Save Update". The note is timestamped and added to the item's history. The "Days Since Update" counter resets to Today.

During the weekly review meeting, go through each stale item (red indicators) and add updates to keep the register current. This ensures the dashboard KPIs accurately reflect the team's risk posture.

Filtering & Searching

Use the filter bar above the register table to quickly find the items you need.

Filter buttons for quick views, plus the search bar for text-based filtering

Available filters

  • All Open — Shows all non-closed items (default view)
  • Stale Items — Shows only items not updated in 14+ days (the count is shown on the button)
  • My Items — Shows only items assigned to you (uses your signed-in name in Live Mode)
  • High Priority / Medium Priority / Low Priority — Filter by priority level

Searching

The search box next to the filter buttons lets you type any keyword to search across all item topics. Results update instantly as you type.

7

Exporting to CSV

Export the full issue tracker to a CSV file for reporting, offline use, or importing into a SharePoint List.

The Export CSV button in the filter bar area

How to export

1

Click the "Export CSV" button in the top-right area of the Issue Tracker section.

2

A CSV file named OFR_Risk_Register_YYYY-MM-DD.csv downloads automatically.

3

Open in Excel, Google Sheets, or import directly into a SharePoint List.

The CSV export includes all fields and all update history in a denormalized format (one row per update). It uses UTF-8 encoding with BOM for proper character display in Excel, including French accented characters.

Bilingual Support (EN / FR)

The entire application is available in both English and French, reflecting PwC Canada's bilingual requirements.

The complete interface displayed in French after toggling the language

Switching languages

Click the FR button in the top-right corner of the header to switch to French. Click EN to switch back to English. The toggle is available on every screen, including the login page.

When in French mode:

  • All labels, buttons, and headings are translated
  • KPI card titles update (e.g., "Open Items" becomes "Elements ouverts")
  • Status labels translate (e.g., "Active" becomes "Actif")
  • The footer and system messages also switch language
Your data (risk topics, owner names, update notes) is not translated — it stays in whatever language it was entered in. Only the application interface switches languages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about using the Risk Tracker.

Where is my data stored?

In Live Mode, all data is stored in SharePoint Online Lists within the PwC M365 tenant. In Demo Mode, data is temporary and stored only in your browser session — it resets when you close the tab.

Who can see the data?

Only users with access to the SharePoint site can view or edit risk data. Access is managed by the OFR team through standard SharePoint permissions.

Can I use this on my phone?

The application is responsive and works on tablets and phones, but it's designed primarily for desktop use during weekly review meetings.

What if SharePoint is down?

The application automatically falls back to Demo Mode if it can't connect to SharePoint. You'll see an error banner with options to retry or switch to demo data. No data is lost — SharePoint has its own redundancy.

How do I get access?

Submit a request through ServiceNow to be added to the OFR Risk Tracker SharePoint site. Your request will be approved by the OFR Risk Lead.

Who do I contact for help?

Reach out to the OFR Risk & Quality team via the #ofr-risk-tracker Teams channel, or email the OFR support mailbox.
